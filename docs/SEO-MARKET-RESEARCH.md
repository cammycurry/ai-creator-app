## TL;DR

There are two massive, overlapping markets we can own:

1. **AI Influencer Creators** — people building AI personas to make money on Instagram, TikTok, and Fanvue. Hustle-driven, money-motivated, stitching together 4-6 tools manually. They want an all-in-one solution.
2. **AI UGC for Brands** — brands replacing $200/video human UGC creators with $5/video AI-generated ads. 93% of brands say UGC outperforms branded content. The AI UGC tool market is exploding.

Both audiences land on different keywords but need the same core product: create a consistent AI person → generate content as them. Our SEO and page content should target both.

## Pain Points (From Reddit, BHW, Forums)

These are the actual complaints people have — what we should address in copy:

### For AI Influencer Creators
1. **"Face consistency is impossible"** — the #1 complaint. People spend hours trying to get the same face across images. Midjourney character refs help but aren't reliable. Flux LoRAs are technical. **We solve this.**
2. **"I need 5 different tools"** — Midjourney for images, Kling for video, ElevenLabs for voice, CapCut for editing, then metadata tools. Expensive, slow, fragmented. **We solve this.**
3. **"It's not passive income"** — 3-10 reels/day, daily chatting on Fanvue, constant content. People underestimate the work. **We make it faster, not passive — honest messaging.**
4. **"AI detection is killing my accounts"** — platforms flagging AI content, metadata giving it away. **We solve this with metadata stripping.**
5. **"I can't make videos that look real"** — lip sync quality is the weakest link in most pipelines. **Opportunity when we ship video.**

### For UGC / Brand Users
1. **"UGC creators are too expensive"** — $150-$2,000/video, plus ghosting, missed deadlines, inconsistent quality. **AI = $5/video, instant, reliable.**
2. **"I need to test 50 variations"** — A/B testing creative at scale is impossible with human creators. **AI generates unlimited variations.**
3. **"Stock AI avatars look fake"** — Creatify/HeyGen avatars are obviously AI. **Custom creators look more authentic.**
4. **"I need product shots with a real-looking person"** — e-commerce product integration, try-ons, unboxings. **Reference upload system planned.**

## Content / Copy Direction

### Core Messaging Hierarchy

**Primary message (hero, ads, first impression):**
> "One tool to create and monetize AI influencers"

**Supporting messages:**
1. "Replace your 6-tool stack" — pain point (cost, complexity)
2. "Same face, every time" — technical differentiator (consistency)
3. "No AI fingerprints" — unique feature (metadata stripping)
4. "From zero to posting in 60 seconds" — speed/ease

### Tone
- **Direct, not corporate.** Talk like the BHW/Reddit community talks — these are hustlers, not enterprise buyers
- **Show the money.** Lead with income potential, not features. "$500K in 10 months" > "Advanced AI generation"
- **Be honest about the work.** Don't promise passive income. Promise faster, cheaper, more consistent content
- **Use their language.** "AI influencer", "OFM", "niche page", "Fanvue", "carousel", "talking head"

### What NOT to Say
- Don't use internal model names (Nano Banana Pro, Gemini, Kling)
- Don't lead with technology — lead with outcomes
- Don't promise "unlimited" or "passive" — promise "faster" and "cheaper"
- Don't be sleazy — there's already enough "make $100K with AI" clickbait. Be real.

## SEO Technical Recommendations

### JSON-LD Schema (Add to Pages)
- **Landing:** `SoftwareApplication` (already exists) + `FAQPage`
- **Workspace gate articles:** `HowTo` schema (step-by-step guides)
- **Blog posts:** `Article` schema with `author`, `datePublished`
- **Pricing section:** `Offer` schema with pricing tiers

### Internal Linking Strategy
- Every page gates → links to other workspace pages (cross-link boost)
- Blog posts → link to workspace pages (funnel from content to product)
- Workspace pages → link to relevant blog posts (depth)
- All gate articles → link to sign-up (conversion)

### Page Speed / Core Web Vitals
- Gate pages should be mostly server-rendered (static SEO content)
- Dummy dashboard can be client-rendered (below the fold for crawlers)
- Images: use next/image with proper sizing, lazy loading below fold
- Hero creator images: optimize to WebP, serve from S3 CDN

### Sitemap Expansion
Current sitemap: landing, sign-up, sign-in, workspace, billing
**Add:** workspace/library, workspace/settings, blog posts (when built)
