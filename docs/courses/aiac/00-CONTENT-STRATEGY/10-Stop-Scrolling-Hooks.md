Now let's talk about the most important thing in content in general, and that is your hook. Because no matter how good your content is, if you don't stop the scroll, you're basically invisible. It doesn't matter if you use AI, faceless b-rolls, or any specific style of video. If you don't manage to stop the scroll, you are invisible. Even with AI, you will still be invisible. So how can we stop the scroll? First of all, you have to understand that even if you love the way your reel looks, how much effort you put into it, the editing, the style, people don't care. People don't care who you are, what you have to say, or how long it took you to make something. When they're scrolling through social media, they only care about three things. If they're looking for entertainment, are they finding it? If they're looking for a solution to a problem, are they finding it? Is the content relevant or valuable to them? Nobody scrolls social media thinking, who can I support today? Not even you or me. We scroll either for entertainment or to find something valuable or helpful. Another important thing, many people scrolling are not sitting at home with full attention. They're scrolling in traffic, on a work break, while walking, on the toilet, during lunch, on public transport, while out with friends, and so on. So two things happen. Most won't have sound on. Most aren't fully focused. This means it's incredibly difficult to stop the scroll, because by the time someone reaches your reel, they've already scrolled past 5, 10, 20, or even 50 pieces of content. So what makes you stand out? When it comes to hooks, there are three types, visual hooks, audio hooks, textual hooks. And a lot of the time, you want to use all three to give yourself the best chance of stopping the scroll. For example, since many people scroll without sound, your visuals must be strong. With AI, this is actually an advantage, because we can now create extremely realistic visuals. Sometimes, the realism alone is enough to stop someone from scrolling. What I like to do, if I use text on screen or lip sync, I always include captions. This gives me a textual hook. I include background music to elevate the video. This gives me an audio hook. I make my avatar's movements and presence as realistic as possible. This is the visual hook. You have to think like this because no one is entitled to watch your content. Just because you spent 3 hours making a reel doesn't mean it will get more than 100 views. And this can be frustrating. But often, the problem isn't the content. It's the hook. Below the video, I will include some links that show variations of hooks and a list of hook types. Not necessarily to copy-paste, though you can, but to help you understand what kinds of hooks actually work. So when creating content, think about your ideal customer and their pain points. Follow the ABC method, Attention, Body, CTA. Reinforce the A by using a strong hook.
