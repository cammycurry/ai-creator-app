When it comes to inspiration, I've always said that the way I find it is by thinking about who my ideal client is, but also by scrolling through Reels, seeing what you guys are doing, what other niches are doing, and taking inspiration from there. Sometimes I like to show the power of AI and what it can do. Sometimes I like to make lip-sync videos talking about certain topics. Sometimes I like short, snappy videos. Basically, I like variety so I can showcase different things. But it's really important not to chase variety constantly. Doing that is exhausting. I actually recommend finding a content style you can easily repeat every day if needed. For example, in my case, that's an image of Maddie with a little microphone talking directly to the camera. No cuts, no B-roll, nothing complicated. Just Maddie speaking, captions, maybe background music, and done. These kinds of videos may not bring massive reach, but if someone watches until the end, the likelihood of them following the call to action is extremely high. No one watches 30 to 50 seconds of some random Reel unless they are genuinely interested in the topic after I grab their attention with my hook. So while these videos can produce less reach, they often produce better conversion. On the other hand, short, fast, snappy clips may get more reach. People might watch them multiple times, but they often convert less. You need both types of content because they balance each other. You cannot post only conversion content if you have no audience yet. So you need reach-focused content and conversion-focused content. That balance is essential. When I first started, every few days I made a simple text-on-screen Reel saying something like, I replaced myself with AI this many days ago and here's what happened. These videos took me five minutes to create and converted extremely well. People love transformation, journeys, timelines, and numbers, and these Reels require almost zero creative energy. So if you can find a content format like that, easy to make, low brainpower, but effective, keep it in your rotation. For example, some creators excel with visuals and always use steal-my-prompts style carousels, which perform incredibly well. They're simple to make, five images, some prompts created with help from ChatGPT or another tool, a strong hook, and a CTA like, comment prompts and I'll send them to you. These get great engagement. The only downside is that most leads will already be people inside AI niche, meaning you may not attract non-AI audiences. But they're still excellent for boosting reach and engagement. Another thing I always do, when I scroll past a Reel I love, I save it to recreate later. You have to understand, everything online already exists. It's extremely hard to invent something completely new. What makes you different is your avatar, how they look, your delivery, how you communicate, your story, real or invented. And that's why we go back to the importance of connection and storytelling. Whether you're sharing your real story through your avatar or you've created a story for them, people need to connect with you. That connection is what later becomes trust and trust is what leads to sales.
