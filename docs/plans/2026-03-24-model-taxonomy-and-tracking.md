### The Real Revenue Split for Fanvue/OFM

- **Subscriptions ($5-$20/mo):** Only 10-20% of revenue — the hook
- **DM chatting with whales:** 50-60% of revenue — parasocial relationship
- **PPV / custom content:** 20-30% of revenue — locked images at $50-$1,000 each
- One documented whale spent **$13k in 30 days**

**Real numbers (BlackHatWorld + Fanvue data):**
- Beginners: $200-$500/mo first months
- Intermediate: $2.5k-$5k/mo
- Serious: $10k-$20k/mo per model
- Top AI creators: $30k-$60k/mo
- Multi-model agencies: $100k-$500k/mo (8-20 models)

**Platform:** Fanvue (NOT OnlyFans — OF bans AI). Fanvue: $100M ARR, 17M MAU, 250k creators, AI = ~15% of GMV.

### "iPhone Casual" — The Most Important Aesthetic

This is #1 for OFM money. The point is to look like a real person's camera roll, NOT professional:
- Slightly off-center framing
- Mixed/imperfect lighting (window + room light, phone flash)
- Everyday settings (bedroom, bathroom mirror, kitchen, car)
- Casual poses (not "modeled" — "texting my friends" energy)
- No visible color grading or filters
- This is what our pipeline already does with "raw iPhone photography"

## What Makes Accounts Succeed or Fail

### The 7 Things Top Accounts Do

1. **Defined personality, not just looks.** Backstory, opinions, interests. Most failures are just pretty faces.
2. **Visual consistency.** 5-7 looks rotated. Same color grading. Recognizable from any single image.
3. **Niche clarity.** Own one lane. Aitana = fitness + gaming. Imma = Tokyo fashion.
4. **iPhone casual > professional.** On Fanvue, over-polished content performs WORSE.
5. **The "innocent to wild" contrast.** For OFM, the escalation is the #1 money printer.
6. **Video content.** Reels get 3x engagement. Static-only plateaus.
7. **Cultural specificity.** Tied to a city (Barcelona, Tokyo, Seoul, LA) > generic person from nowhere.

### The 6 Reasons Accounts Fail

1. Generic beauty with no personality
2. Inconsistent face between posts
3. No niche focus
4. Over-polished when niche needs "real" energy
5. Post and ghost — no engagement strategy
6. Trying to hide AI origin

## Phase Plan

### Phase 1: Seed (10-15 accounts)
Manually collect known accounts listed above. Establish data format, calibrate ratings.

### Phase 2: Expand (50+ accounts)
Semi-automated (Instaloader/Apify). Every niche needs 3+ accounts. Prioritize girl-next-door and glamour.

### Phase 3: Classify (AI-Assisted)
Run images through Claude/GPT-4V to auto-fill format, pose, lighting, quality. Human review.

### Phase 4: Extract → App
- Map top accounts → premade creator archetypes
- Extract best compositions → composition templates
- Build aesthetic → prompt mapping
- Build monetization-aware content templates
- Expand premade library to 50+

## Notes

- Fanvue/OFM is the elephant in the room. Users WILL make adult content. Design the tool to serve this without being explicitly adult.
- Male niches are unproven for AI but worth including — someone will crack it.
- "iPhone Casual" is THE aesthetic to nail — it's what makes the money niches work.
- Faceless/UGC needs video (lip sync) which we don't have yet. Image-first still works.
- VTubers are a different tech (animated, not photorealistic) but the same market — watch for convergence.
- Regional markets are real — Latina, Asian, Black are all standalone niches with dedicated audiences.
- The couple/pet/parenting niches are blue ocean — hard to execute with current AI but first-mover advantage is huge.
- This taxonomy is v3. Will evolve as we collect data and see what patterns emerge in the wild.
